Project Statement

“In Unstable Motion” draws inspiration from the Manifesto for the Unstable Media, which states, “Unstable media are characterised by dynamic motion and changeability.” This quote resonated with me, as it aligns with my perspective on technological development and the constantly shifting landscape of new media and the internet. As a designer focused on AI and technology, I see this instability as a reflection of today’s evolving digital environment. In this work, I use the pendulum as a metaphor to explore the chaotic and unpredictable nature of new media and technological advancement. The artwork centres on a double pendulum, a well-known symbol of chaos in physics, representing unpredictability and instability. Its motion mirrors the way digital media constantly changes, where no trajectory can be predicted. This reflects the fluid, ever-changing nature of communication and information in the digital age. The central quote, presented in both English and its Chinese translation, appears as fluctuating text across the canvas, using varied fonts,
sizes, and colours to visually convey the instability of modern
digital communication.

In this piece, users become active participants in the chaos. They can introduce additional pendulums, which add layers of dynamic, ever-changing text to the artwork, symbolising the exponential growth of the internet and the fragmented nature of contemporary communication. As these pendulums swing and interact, they create a dense, chaotic visual, reflecting the overwhelming flow of information in the digital space. The pendulums gradually fill the canvas, creating a circle representing how digital text and information are bound by the world of web of the internet. While users can delete pendulums, the marks they leave behind remain a metaphor for the permanence of digital footprints, highlighting the inescapable impact of our actions in the virtual world.

At its core, this artwork critiques the unpredictable and sometimes chaotic nature of digital media, but it also celebrates its creative potential. The pendulums embody the tension between chaos and innovation, reflecting how technology can both disrupt and inspire. Through this piece, I aim to depict the digital landscape as a space of instability, but also one of endless creative possibilities. Beyond the new media art, teachnology of AI, for example, can cause job displacement, particularly in lower-skilled sectors. However, its efficiency and potential for innovation make it an inevitable part of our future.

Paper Prototype

My initial concept for the paper prototype was to begin with a single black pendulum, symbolising traditional media, which would contrast with the subsequent bobs representing new media. The initial pendulum would remain static in its features, staying black, while any additional pendulums introduced by the user would change dynamically. Users would have the ability to add an infinite number of pendulums, enhancing interactivity and visual complexity.

However, I soon realised that this approach posed significant challenges in terms of coding. As a result, I refined the design to feature a double pendulum as the starting point. Users can now add or remove additional double pendulums from the same origin, with a limited frame size, making the system more manageable from a coding perspective while still maintaining interactivity and aesthetic.
